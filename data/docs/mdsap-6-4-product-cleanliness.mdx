<RegLang>
Determine if the medical device organization has established documented requirements for product cleanliness including any cleaning prior to sterilization, cleanliness requirements if provided non-sterile, and assuring that process agents are removed from the product if required.

Brazil(ANVISA): Confirm that a pest control program has been established and where chemicals are used as part of the pest 
control program, the company must ensure that they do not affect product quality. Verify that the manufacturer has established and maintains housekeeping procedures and schedules for production areas and warehouses, in conformance with production specifications.
</RegLang>

<Subtask actionitem={"Review cleanliness requirements in product specifications."}>
Cleanliness requirements are to be established in design documentation, likely a product specification. This is to controlled as part of the design and above the manufacturing work instruction level. 
Link to <QLink>MDSAP Task 6-10 Monitoring and Measurement of Product Conformity</QLink>: Ensure that the design cleanliness specifications have been appropriately carried forward to the production documents for acceptance activities.
</Subtask>

<Subtask actionitem={"Review pest control processes and record."}>
While this has a stronger link to <QLink>MDSAP Task 6-6</QLink>, it can be documented in either task. Review typically involves review of a pest control map.  Ensure pest control records contain notes on pest activity found.
</Subtask>
